How We Do This

It all starts with our global network. We've been building it for over 10 years. We've spent hundreds of millions on the footprint to get us within 50 milliseconds of 95% of the users on the Internet.

Our infrastructure is in data centers in over 275 cities around the world across more than 100 countries.

Why so many cities? First, that's where the Internet population is. But more importantly, when it comes to performance and security, you can't improve upon the speed of light. We're constantly thinking about how we can bring the best experience as close as possible to the end user eyeballs, to provide the lowest latency and highest possible performance. On the security side, we want to be able to detect and mitigate threats where they happen and stop them in their tracks.

Network Interconnections

It's not just about data centers and cities - it's also about the interconnections. Today, we connect directly with over 11,000 networks, constantly building and strengthening that fabric.

We're making sure that we not only have a big and broad network, but that it's also incredibly powerful to handle massive amounts of traffic and to mitigate and detect massive threats. At the end of 2023, our overall capacity was 155 Tbps, and that's growing everyday.
