How do we do this?

It all starts with our global network. We’ve been building it for over 10 years. We’ve spent hundreds of millions on the footprint to get us within 50 milliseconds of 95% of the users on the Internet. And we continue to make this one of our top priorities. 

Our  infrastructure is in data centers in over 275 cities around the world across more than 100 countries. 

Why so many cities? Well, first of all, that's where the Internet population is. We're global. But more importantly, we appreciate that at the end of the day, when it comes to performance and security, you can't improve upon the speed of light. We're constantly thinking about how we can bring the best experience as close as possible to the end user eyeballs, to provide the lowest latency and highest possible performance. On the security side, we want to be able to detect and mitigate threats where they happen and stop them in their tracks.

Now, it's not just about those data centers and the different cities. It's also about the interconnections, or the connections that we have to those different networks. Today, we connect directly with over 11,000 networks, and we're constantly building on top of that to build and strengthen that fabric. And, of course, under all of this, we're making sure that we not only have a big and broad network, but that it's also incredibly powerful to handle massive amounts of traffic and to mitigate and detect massive threats.

At the end of 2023, our overall capacity was 155 Tbps, and that’s growing everyday. We are servicing millions of customers and adding new websites and apps every day.
